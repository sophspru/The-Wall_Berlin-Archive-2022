Berlin 2022, Personal Archive

Since I was little, I love stories. For this reason, I like to keep train tickets, receipts, flyers, during the trips I take, because their stories help me relive the moments I've passed and remember the people I've met. Start studying in the field of visual communication, I realized how interesting it was to preserve paper artifacts from a place different from mine; both the positive examples and the curious ones still constitute solutions to communication problems by someone other than me and from whom I can learn something. In 2022 I had the chance to study for one semester in Berlin. The impact with a place so different from the one I came from was immediately a constant source of inspiration, which intrigued me. Right from the start, I started storing postcards, tickets, stickers, handwritten notes, in a box. Once back, that box was a nostalgic collection of memories and a small personal archive of my experience.

Five months in a box

Elements

The elements I have collected are of various nature and origin, both paper and small objects. To represent them in my archive, I decided to proceed by scanning everything that was paper and photographing the objects in 3D, and then proceeding for both categories with a contour cut in Photoshop.

Layout and style choices

Berlin is an eclectic city, multicultural and with various influences. Since the items of the archive are already reflecting this, I decided to keep a neutral background, using a picture of concrete. The grey color doesn't interfere with the colors of the elements, allowing their readability. I choose concrete also to connect this archive to the imagery of a wall, a symbol of division for the city, that right now - as for my archive - represent connection to the past, memories. I wanted to add an horizonal scroll instead of the vertical one, to create a sense of disconfort for the user, the same disconfort I had when I first arrived in Berlin, but I didn't managed to.

Regarding the text, at first I wanted to recreate the buttons and the title in a graffiti style, as if they were done on the wall. Since I couldn't manage to make the font work, I decided to use "Berlin", a font created by the HvD foundry, based on the corporate identity created for the city in 2020 by Jung von Matt. It is a font studied starting from the entire city signage, which therefore combines ancient and modern.

For the title, I opted for a blue background and white, as a reminder for the metro (Ubahn) of Berlin.

The rest of the text (on the buttons and on the image description) are a palette of three color: white, black and yellow, identifying the city. The only button that is different from the others is the "shuffle" one; the word is replaced by the "S" logo of the urban train line.

Interactions

The buttons at the top are filters, which allow you to view a precise category of elements. I have decided on a subdivision into 2D or 3D and for each of these two categories, I have foreseen at least one further division.

Next to the filters, it is possible to select the display order of the items: in ascending or descending order of collection date.

Thanks to the shuffle button at the top left, it is possible to randomly mix the elements.

Thanks to the search bar, you can find a specific item by typing a keyword.

By clicking on an item in the archive, it is possible to enlarge it and view its title.

To move from one element to the next, once selected, I tried to train Google's Teachable Machine to recognize images, namely the iconic Ampelmann (the little man depicted on the pedestrian traffic light). Unfortunately I ran into some trouble setting up the code and couldn't get it to work.

As an alternative, I've replaced this method with using the keyboard: typing the letter "p" takes you back to the previous item, typing "n" takes you to the next item.

Final result and conclusions

Berlin has been a city that over time I have greatly appreciated, in all its positive and negative aspects. I plan to go back there very often and continue my collection and consequently also the archive. It will be even more interesting to see in a few years what I have collected.
